Elana's Insights
Key takeaways from your May 31st call. Come back here when you need a reset.
Insight 1 — The Differentiator
AI experience overrides everything else on a CV right now.
Her director said anyone with hands-on AI project experience jumped to the top regardless of everything else. This isn't a nice-to-have. It's the filter.
Insight 2 — n8n > Claude for standing out
Claude is "easy." n8n is the edge.
Prompt-based tools are table stakes. n8n requires you to connect APIs and think architecturally. That's what hiring managers get excited about. Build there first.
Insight 3 — The Shift Is Real
MOPs as a title is being phased out. GTM Engineer is what's next.
Her CMO confirmed it. The role is evolving — more AI-focused, more technical. If you get a standard MOPs role today, it may look completely different in 12–24 months. Position for the direction, not the current label.
Insight 4 — The Real Bottleneck
Your presentation is likely the issue — not your skills.
You know you can do the work. You're just not communicating it convincingly. The gap isn't capability — it's articulation. Lead with what you'd do, not just what you've done.
Insight 5 — Vision Over History
"If I were in this role, here's what I'd build" is more powerful than a resume recitation.
Especially for GTM engineer / AI MOPs roles where nobody has the exact experience. Coming in with a concrete vision of what you'd improve is what "creativity" means to hiring managers.
Insight 6 — Apply Above Yourself
50% of people in their roles aren't qualified for them.
Her sister's rule — and Elana lives by it. The market runs on confidence and fit, not perfect credential matching. You already proved this at Cognyte. Do it again.
Insight 7 — Gap Questions Are Tests
When they push on what you haven't done, they may be testing how you handle pressure — not disqualifying you.
Have the framework ready: "I haven't done X yet — but here's what I have done, and here's why I know I can." Confidence under pressure is the signal they're looking for.
Insight 8 — The Best Tip of the Call
Define one clear unique selling point. Make it the centerpiece of everything.
Not a list of skills — one thing that makes you different. Then your CV, your pitch, and every interview answer builds around it. Everything else is noise until you nail this.
Insight 9 — Stepping Stone Strategy
A short pivot role to build missing AI experience is a legitimate path — not a step down.
Elana did it herself. Went to a company she didn't want to work for, got the skills she needed, moved up. If the AI experience gap keeps blocking you, a cloud/AI integration role (even 6 months) may be the faster route to the GTM engineer title.
