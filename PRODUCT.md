# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Anyone actively repositioning in tech — primarily people making the jump from traditional GTM/MOPs roles into AI-native or GTM Engineering work. They use this during their job search: sharpening their pitch, building skills, logging portfolio work, and preparing for interviews. They open it with intent, not casually. Context: focused, time-pressured, self-directed.

## Product Purpose

A personal command center for a high-stakes job search. Not a resume builder or a job tracker. A place to sharpen your edge: define your USP, prove your AI skills are real, prep answers to the hard questions, and execute a learning plan. Success looks like walking into interviews with a clear, confident story and a portfolio that backs it up.

## Brand Personality

Sharp. Forward. Earned.

Voice: direct, no hedging, no cheerleading. Talks to you like a peer who takes the work seriously. Momentum-forward: every element should make you feel like you're making progress, not just organizing information.

## Anti-references

- **Job boards and recruiting tools** (LinkedIn, Greenhouse, Lever, Workday): corporate blue, form-heavy layouts, resume-builder aesthetic, friendly-HR tone, excessive white space. This should feel nothing like a system you're submitting to — it's a system you command.
- **Generic career coaching apps**: motivational quotes, pastel palettes, soft-rounded UI, gamified badge systems.

## Design Principles

1. **Evidence over aspiration.** Every section should feel like it's building toward something real and demoable. Not "learning n8n" — showing what you've built. Design that emphasizes proof over intent.
2. **Pressure without noise.** High-energy doesn't mean busy. Urgency should come from clarity and forward momentum, not decoration.
3. **The tool respects your intelligence.** No hand-holding copy, no gratuitous tooltips, no empty-state placards that say "nothing here yet 🙁". Treat the user as someone who knows what they're doing.
4. **Personal, not generic.** The design and content should feel like it was made for and by someone specific — not a template anyone could plug their name into.
5. **Ship-quality.** The UI should match the claim. If the page says you build high-quality AI tools, the page itself needs to demonstrate that.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Keyboard navigable throughout. Reduced-motion alternative for all animations. No information conveyed by color alone.
